The appendix is like a little pocket at the start of your large intestine! It's shaped like a tiny tube (about 2-4 inches long).

For a long time, doctors thought it was just a leftover part from our ancestors that didn't do anything special - kind of like your tonsils!

üåü The Appendix's Secret Superpower!

Scientists now think your appendix has an important job - it keeps your gut healthy by storing good bacteria!

When you get sick (like with diarrhea), some good bacteria get flushed away. Your appendix helps reboot your digestive system by releasing stored good bacteria.

Sometimes the appendix can get blocked and infected - this is called appendicitis. When this happens:

  • It swells up like a balloon with too much air
  • It can cause bad tummy pain (especially on the lower right side)
  • Doctors might need to remove it with surgery

Good news: People can live perfectly fine without an appendix!
